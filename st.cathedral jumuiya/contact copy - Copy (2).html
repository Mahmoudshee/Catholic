--St.Maria--

Saint Mary, the Mother of God, holds a unique and exalted position within the Catholic Church, embodying virtues that serve as a model for all believers, particularly women. Her exemplary holiness encourages the faithful to "raise their eyes to Mary who shines forth before the whole community of the elect as a model of the virtues" such as faith, obedience, humility, and charity.

Maryâ€™s faith is particularly highlighted; she welcomed the angel Gabriel's message with complete trust, exemplifying the obedience of faith. This is encapsulated in her response, "Behold I am the handmaid of the Lord; let it be [done] to me according to your word." Her unwavering belief that "nothing will be impossible with God" has led generations to call her blessed.

Moreover, Mary is recognized as a model of perfect discipleship and a mother for the Church. Her life reflects a deep and unconditional adherence to God's will, making her a preeminent member of the Church and a unique realization of its mission. The Church honors her as a guiding figure, especially during challenging times, as she is seen as closely united with the Church in its struggles.

Saint Mary is celebrated for several virtues that reflect her profound faith and role within the Church:

1. Faith: Mary trusted in God's plan and welcomed the angel Gabriel's message with full confidence, saying, "Let it be done to me according to your word."
2. Obedience: Her life exemplifies perfect obedience to God's will, from the Annunciation to her presence at the Crucifixion.
3. Humility: Mary referred to herself as the "handmaid of the Lord," showing her deep humility in serving God's plan.
4. Charity: She visited Elizabeth to provide support during her pregnancy, demonstrating her selfless love and care for others.
5. Model of Discipleship: Mary lived her life as a perfect disciple, embodying the mission and values of the Church in all her actions.
